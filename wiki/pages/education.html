Introduction

Countless examples demonstrate that receiving a good education is crucial for a brighter future. This principle applies not only to individuals but also to countries and communities. Our alma mater, Lanzhou University, is situated in northwestern China, an area characterized by a relatively desolate environment and an economic development level that pales in comparison to more prosperous regions such as Shanghai or Guangdong. Additionally, our inland location results in limited transportation options. These factors contribute to a less than satisfactory level of educational development here. In the context of globalization, coastal regions are flourishing, while our inland area appears to have a bleak outlook. It seems our university has little hope of reclaiming its status as one of China's top ten institutions. Our circumstances and the resources available to us mirror those of individuals from other inland regions and countries. In a global landscape that thrives on maritime trade, people from inland areas struggle to access the same resources as those in coastal regions across various domains, including economics, trade, technology, and education.

One fateful night, in the quiet corridors of our educational journey, a conversation unfolded that would alter the course of our lives:

“Is there an inherent barrier for people from inland regions when it comes to education, compared to those from coastal areas? Does having fewer resources make it harder for us, as inland dwellers, to succeed?” a team member mused, his voice filled with frustration.

Another replied, “Perhaps we don’t always need to chase the highest honors. We can become our own role models through hard work, carve out our unique identities, and set an example in our region. While we may not be able to provide more people with access to high-quality educational resources, by developing a full range of educational activities aimed at the widest possible audience. That is already a meaningful contribution!”

Thus, we decided to embark on a comprehensive science popularization journey. We believe that the dissemination of scientific knowledge should be inclusive, targeting all age groups and reaching as many people as possible.

Our journey spans four distinct levels: schools, communities and cities, the nation, and the globe, each presenting unique challenges and opportunities. At the school level, we focused on two specific age groups: children and elementary school students, university students.

In schools, we organized fun science games for children, laboratory tours, scientific conferences for university students, and slide painting activities. These initiatives captivated young minds and encouraged the artistic expression of complex scientific concepts.

In Lanzhou, Gansu Province, we collaborated with community service centers and hospitals to host science popularization activities for audiences of all ages, primarily featuring engaging games and informative presentations.

On a national scale, we tailored educational programs to address regional disparities and distributed printed cancer science handbooks across the country.

Globally, we developed and disseminated multilingual cancer knowledge handbooks, shared science articles on WeChat public accounts, and created a manual advocating for educational equity. We are committed to ensuring that the dissemination of scientific knowledge faces no barriers, whether related to national borders, natural conditions, age, or gender.

By adopting a precision-oriented approach, we have ensured that everyone—regardless of age, ethnicity, gender, or background—can access scientific knowledge. We successfully integrated art and science, conducting educational activities in engaging ways that unite hearts and minds around the world.

Our view of education

In the vision of our team, education has been endowed with a more profound and rich connotation, which goes far beyond the level of simple knowledge indoctrination and skill teaching, and becomes a profound spiritual journey and a feast of wisdom awakening. We firmly believe that the true meaning of education, as the French Enlightenment thinker Rousseau-put it, is not only to sow the seeds of knowledge in the hearts of students, but also to ignite their inner flame of exploring the unknown and pursuing the truth, leading them to embark on an extraordinary journey of self-discovery and growth.

The Chinese sage Lao Zi once said, "It is better to teach people to fish than to teach them to fish." As a group of explorers with scientific dreams and educational enthusiasm, we are committed to building an open, inclusive and innovative learning environment, so that every student can breathe freely on this fertile soil and explore bravingly. We believe that the power of education lies in inspiring students' own learning potential. Through the project-driven learning model, students are encouraged to think independently, practice, and combine theoretical knowledge with practical problems, so as to deepen understanding in practice, sharpen their will in challenges, and learn to communicate and collaborate in cooperation.

On the platform of iDEC, we encourage students to have the courage to question and challenge authority, and guide every member to pay attention to social hotspots, think about science and technology ethics, examine the world from a scientific perspective, and warm people's hearts with humanistic feelings. In various community activities, we apply the knowledge we have learned to help others, not only helping the residents of the community, but also educating each team member themselves. Because we attach great importance to spiritual touch and enlightenment, the ultimate goal of education is to cultivate future leaders who are capable of independent thinking, social responsibility and humanistic care. We believe that such education can cultivate interdisciplinary talents with both profound scientific literacy and noble moral sentiments, so as to contribute to the progress and development of society.

To sum up, iDEC team's viewpoint and purpose in education is to pay more attention to the enlightenment of mind and the awakening of wisdom while imbuing knowledge. Through innovative education methods, students' inner motivation is stimulated, and they are trained to become new era youth with innovative spirit, practical ability, social responsibility and humanistic care. We firmly believe that this kind of education is the true sense of education, which can not only illuminate the future of students, but also illuminate the direction of human civilization.

Cross-national dialogue to explore diet and health

The association between food habits and type 2 diabetes

Through comparative analysis, we found that there were significant differences in the proportion of carbohydrates, fat, protein and other nutrients in food habits in different regions, which directly affected people's blood glucose levels and the incidence of type 2 diabetes. For example, high sugar and high fat dietary patterns in some regions increase the risk of type 2 diabetes, while diets rich in dietary fiber and low in sugar and fat can help prevent and control the disease.
